Al Gore’s 5 best lines about the internet

Al Gore likes to talk about the internet and is conversant with its language. The former vice president turned a phrase Monday when he told a crowd at SXSW in Austin, Texas: “Our democracy has been hacked.” Here’s POLITICO’s look back at some of the more amusing things the former vice president has said (often in jest) over the years about the Internet and computers:

1. “During my service in the United States Congress, I took the initiative in creating the Internet.” (March 1999)

2. “The day I made that statement, about the inventing the Internet, I was tired because I’d been up all night inventing the camcorder.” (April 1999)

3. “How could [Y2K] be a problem in a country where we have Intel and Microsoft?” (Jan. 1999)

4. “Our democracy, our constitutional framework is really a kind of software for harnessing the creativity and political imagination for all of our people. The American democratic system was an early political version of Napster.” (Sept. 2000)

5. “Remember America, I gave you the Internet and I can take it away.” (Sept. 2000, 9th item on the “Top 10 List of Rejected Gore-Lieberman Campaign Slogans” list on “The Late Show with David Letterman”)
